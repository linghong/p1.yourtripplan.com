My Bio

Hangzhou, My Hometown

I was born in a beautiful town, Hangzhou, a city with tons of modern structure, but also famous for its wealth of history, culture and the beauty of the West Lake. As many of you probably had never been heard of the name, my hometown Hangzhou, located in the middle east coast of China and 110 mi southwest of Shanghai, is actually highly praised by numerous China's ancient poets and writers, and called "the heaven in the earth" in China.

Places with natural beauty are easy to find in the world, however, you won't find another beautiful place in the world that has so many folk stories, so many poems, so many artists' life are with it. For examples, The most two famous and sadly loving stories in China, similar as Romiette and Julio in western countries, were from Hangzhou. You can still find the places where the lovers met and where they were sadly separated. Hangzhou is also one of the seven ancient capitals in China, and the south end of China's ancient Grand Cannel built on AD 689 also stops in Hangzhou.

When I graduated from high school, I studied biology at Zhejiang University, the best college in Hangzhou and also one of the top universities in China. Growing up in such a beautiful place, I had never planned leaving it permanently till I studied the graduate school at Chinese Academy of Sciences in Shanghai, and started to be influenced by my schoolmates whom almost all went to USA after graduated.

My Road to USA

Soon after I graduated, I found a job in USA. However, my road to USA was very bumpy. I failed in visa application. Unlike today, US Consulate generals are very welcome Chinese, getting a US visa was a very tough thing at that time. Having failed the visa application several times, I decided to try another country. Thus I came to Berlin. Berlin is an attractive city. People are very friendly. I still remember my first morning in Berlin.

Even in a place where lots of students had gone abroad, the information about Germany was still scarce. I mistakenly thought banks would be also everywhere as they were in any big city in China. So I brought US dollars and didn't exchange them into German Mark in China. I arrived at Berlin at late night. The next morning, when I couldn't find a bank to exchange money, I had to bring $20 to a cafeteria to buy breakfast and of course got rejected. A man stood nearby giving the clerk 10 German Mark and told the clerk this would be used to pay for my meal . I asked why, he answered "because you are hungry " and left. This is my first impression about Germany. A very warm welcome from that country.

My job in the lab was easy. German people seem never work latein the day or work in weekend and holidays. I took the advantage of that and visited lots of places in Germany and in Europe. Germany is a nice place, people were very friendly, and weather was also good, nevertheless, as a foreigner, you would always feel you were different from them. The feeling that " I am only a guest and not belonging to the country" always bothered me and eventually brought me to New York.

My life in New York

New York city is dirty, crowded, and noisy. Compare to Germany, people are far less warmhearted. You won't love that kind of place in the beginning, and may even feel a little bit frustrated. However, the longer you live there, the more you'll find the treasures behind it, eventually you will fall in love with it and don't want to leave any more.

In New York city, everyone is like me, coming from different countries either earlier or later and having different skin colors, or even speaking different accent. You can also find almost all the food from your native country that you have been accustomed to for so many years. You won't feel you, as a foreigner, are only a stranger in that country. So I immediately felt home, a home that is not gorgeous and not perfect, but anyway it is yours.

In the working days, I became a busy scientist, doing the research with cultured cells and mice, playing with bottles, dishes and microplates, studying under microscopes, monitoring incubators and equipments, etc. In the weekend, I walked around the city digging deeply its precious assets: the happy kids skating on the snow ground in the winter of the Central Park, the crazy people in New Year's Time Square, the peculiar parades celebrating for their own country's holidays in the fifth avenue, the long lines waiting outside of the cinemas in Broadway, the gorgeous sunset on the blue seashore along the quite beach in Bronx, the amazing Manhattan skyline watched from Brooklyn Heights...... Many people in New York city love to go to bars and clubs at night. I am not a fan of that, but I love the Broadway shows, the theatre, the museums and the tons of exhibitions New York city always provides, and also the feeling of that you can go out to buy things even in the very late of a night.

Digging into the Code World

Two or three years ago, I wanted to build a website for a small business I wanted to try. I didn't have a clear idea how my website should look like. So I didn't want to spend money building a site that I would change my mind and rebuilt it soon. I decided to learn to build a simple site by myself. After doing research, I found Wordpress was the best option for my purpose because it was easy to learn and had an amazing open source CMS with a large community, free themes and lots of plugins.

Months later, I wanted to add some looks on my site that neither the theme nor plugins provided. I did internet search, took whatever I could found in the internet that looked like related to test on my site. So my CSS code was mingled with the html codes. I also didn't have any idea whether they were from html5 or html4 or xhtml. Anyway, whatever worked, I took it.

Gradually, I felt it was too time consuming and I'd better to learn the html and CSS codes. I started to learn them on the internet, but I actually found the best way to learn these codes was reading a new and good book. It gave me much clearer and systemic explanation and has more useful examples than what I could found in the internet. Unexpectedly, the more I am involved in the code world, the more I found I love in building website than the small business I initially tried to work on. I even made my second website, which was still built on wordpress, but this time more codes were written by myself.

I eventually decide to take a class to see how my feeling will be. That is why I am here in this class. If it goes well, I'll take more classes.

My Code Editor

What's code editor I am using? Actually, in the very beginning, I used notepad (not the notepad++). Since I only wrote a few lines of codes to add in my existing wordpress pages, it seemed OK. Later on, I found a free Dreamweaver software in the internet, so I downloaded it and started to use Dreamweaver to write codes. It was only Dreamweaver CS3, but it was still helpful, since if I typed a wrong code the color would be different. Now after attending this course, I am trying Sublime Text2. Hopefully, Sublime Text2 will be better than Dreamweaver.
